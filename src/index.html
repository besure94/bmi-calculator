What is body mass index?

Body mass index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on a person's height and weight. Listed below are the five BMI categories:

BMI Weight Status
Below 18.5 Underweight
18.5 - 24.9 Healthy Weight
25.0 - 29.9 Overweight
30 - 39.9 Obesity
40 or over Severe Obesity

What is obesity?

Obesity is abnormal or excessive fat accumulation that presents a risk to health, as defined by the World Health Organization.

Please note that BMI does not account for all body types, muscle mass, or body fat percentage. For example, a 6'3" 225 lb (190.5cm and 102.1 kg) professional baseball player with high muscle mass and low body fat would have a BMI of 28.1. Their BMI category would classify them as "overweight", despite their excellent physical fitness and athletic ability.

For these reasons, BMI should not be considered the entire measure of a persons health. Please consult a doctor if you are concerned about your weight and/or physical health.
